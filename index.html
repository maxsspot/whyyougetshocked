WHY DO YOU GET SHOCKED WHEN TOUCHING METAL?

Have you ever went to open a door or have you ever picked up a metal object? Chances are, you probably were 'shocked' once or twice!

You have probably wondered what that is or why it happens. If you have, look no further, because I have the answer for you!

Getting shocked involves electrons (e-lek-trons). You, on a normal day, have a lot of electrons. Metal, and things made of metal, on the other hand, do not.

The basic reason you get shocked is because some electrons are jumping from you to the object you are touching.

In conclusion, that is why you may get shocked when touching metal or metal objects.
